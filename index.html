Al-Azhar Park

Al-Azhar Park offers a 72-acre space of greenery in the midst of Old Cairo, with an architectural design inspired by historic Islamic gardens. The spine of the Park is a formal axis with water fountains channeling towards the direction of a small lake, along with alleyways pointing towards the Citadel of Cairo. Al-Azhar Mosque in Cairo: A Tourist's Guide

Al Azhar mosque is one of the oldest mosques in Cairo, Egypt. The Mosque was built in the 10th century by the orders of the Fatimid Caliph Sultan al-Muizz li Din Allah, who commissioned its construction to mark the site of his new capital, Cairo.
